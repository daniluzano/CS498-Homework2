Discussion: Why do you think the latencies differ? 
What does your consistency experiment show?

The latency measurements show a clear difference between the US EU region. For both /register and /list, the latency of the US instance (133.6 ms and 122.3 ms,  respectively) was significantly lower than the latency of the EU instance (457.6 ms and 563.6 ms, respectively).
This difference occurred because of the geographical location from which I ran my script.
Because I executed the script in the United States, requests sent to the us-central1 VM traveled a shorter distance than requests sent to europe-west1.
The higher latency for the EU instance reflects the additional time and distance required for the network to communicate such a long distance.
The occasional spike in time, such as the 1417 ms /list request, was may have been caused by temporary network congestion.

In the eventual consistency experiment, the number of times the new username was not found in the list immediately after registration was zero. In my script, I wrote to the US instance and immediately read from the EU instance.
This suggests that the replication lag between regions is minimal and undetectable in this experiment.
The newly written data was visible almost immediately, even though the system operates under an eventually consistent model, which allows for temporary replication delays.
